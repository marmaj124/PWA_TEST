Zen Water has three tiers of responsibility within our company: Executive, Manager and Individual. Here’s how we break down each level’s duties.


Executive


  • company and product strategy
    company: structure, finances, marketing/publishing, evaluating/adjusting core values and ethics
    product: values, long-term direction, short-term (ZenCycle) direction, marketing coordination
  • product work and communication
    making personal contributions to product work writing heartbeats and kick-offs (with input from the team); providing status updates in between as needed maintaining a consistently visible leadership presence, especially during deadline-driven work
  • people management: admin
    creating and tracking performance improvement plans for employees as needed
  • people management: personal
    continually identifying and addressing potential performance problems in Managers or their team dynamic requesting status updates from teams

Manager


  • (manager is basically a leader of the team - the one who writes kick-offs and heartbeats)
  • team strategy
    driver/delegator of work; “team lead”; ultimately responsible for the team’s output
  • product work and communication
    making personal contributions to product work writing heartbeats and kick-offs (with input from the team), maintaining a consistently visible leadership presence, especially during deadline-driven work
  • people management: admin
    creating and tracking performance improvement plans for the team as needed
  • people management: personal
    continually identifying and addressing potential performance problems in the team anticipating and addressing moral issues keeping the pulse of the emotional well-being of the team, including workload, attachment to work, on the lookout for fatigue or burnout translating team feedback on overall company direction and morale to Executives

Individual


  • product work and communication
    making personal contributions to product work contributing to heartbeats and kick-offs; providing status updates in between as needed interpretation of daily work from the ZenCycle strategy
  • people management: personal
    communicating feedback to Managers on the overall team or company direction and morale
