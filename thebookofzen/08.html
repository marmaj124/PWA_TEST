BASECAMP MANUAL

ASYNCHRONOUS

READING TIME:
5 MINUTES
08 / 14

Most of the work you do at Zen Water shouldn’t require you to be in constant communication throughout the entire day with someone. That’s why we are going asynchronous. We are creating a system that is going to be adopted by millions of other people in the world. And you are one of the first ones trying it! You can take this know-how into your personal projects in the future for sure.


The idea is not that everything we ever decide to work on has to take two or three weeks or can be completed in that time. But rather that we think about how we can break big solutions to our problems into smaller ones that can be done in that amount of time.


It’s far better for everyone’s concentration and sanity if you collaborate as though most things will get an answer eventually, but not necessarily right this second. Your first choice of action should be to post a message, a todo, or a document about what you need to explain or need to know. Then others can read it on their schedule, when the natural lulls of the day allow it, rather than being interrupted right in their peak flow time.


Don’t take that as gospel, though. Sometimes you really DO need to tightly collaborate with someone for an extended period, and that’s fine. We have pings, Around video calls, screen sharing, or even in-person collaboration for when nothing else will do. (But most of the time something else will).


In other words, no one is going to be required to be online at one specific time. You can work on anything Zen Water-related anytime you want if you could fulfil the deadlines for finishing discussed goals at the beginning of each ZenCycle.


In the First Response Team, people must be dependently available when they say they will be. So what applies to almost all teams in the company may well apply a little less frequently in the FRT team.


Everyone is independent


Organizational theory is thick with descriptions of the trade-offs between functional and project company structures. We seek to be more project and only-team based than others. This means that each team should be able to go from idea to deployment as independently as possible. Thus, the fewer teams need to react to the progress on their road to finishing out a new goal, the better.


For example, a team working on a new website feature should be able to test their work without involving the marketing team, unless something special is needed.


You are your own manager


Managing at Zen Water is a part-time occupation, next to being involved with doing the work itself. This means we rely on everyone at Zen Water to do a lot of self-management. That means setting your own direction when one isn’t given. Determining what needs to be done, and doing it, without waiting for someone to tell you to.


The Balance


We limit ourselves to a 40-hour workweek. Keeping our hours at work limited forces us to prioritize the work that really matters. A healthy amount of sleep and rich and rewarding life outside of work should not be destroyed by a few more hours at work.


There are occasions where teams or individuals need to work outside of their working hours’ for on-call, maintenance or emergencies. This time should not be in addition to your normal working hours. Use your discretion to take time off to make up for the additional hours you put in during the week.
