VOCABULARY

READING TIME:
5 MINUTES
03 / 14

Like most other companies in the world, Zen Water has with help of other organisations developed a vocabulary specific to Zen Water. It’s easy to overdo and it’s even easier to do cringeworthy. We don’t want to have a culture of Zen Wateros CRUSHING IT to get to the NEXT LEVEL with some super-duper PEAK PERFORMANCE, BRUH. The vast majority of the time, we can use the vocabulary shared with the rest of the English-speaking world, and we’ll be better off. On the other hand…


Moon
A digital space where you find everything. It's a place, where you put all the content regarding Zen Water, where you have all the tasks which are putting you closer to make your crazy ideas real. This space is open to everyone in Zen Water, everyone can see what is happening on the other side of Zen Water and anyone can react to anything. There is no place to hide there!


Zen Space
A part of the Moon environment is available for everyone in Zen Water. If you post a message or a file in this space (you will learn later about it), everyone in the company will see it.


Judo
Most problems can be solved in a thousand different ways. Turning that massive, scary 1-month looking problem into one that can be done in 1 week instead. It’s often used when we get frustrated trying to solve something hard and we aren’t making sufficient progress: “Let’s figure out a way to judo this!”.


ASAP
This word doesn’t exist in our vocabulary anymore. So don’t use it :) Nothing is that urgent for using this toxic phrase. We care about our time, and we know that 99% don’t need to be solved at that moment. If something goes wrong, we have our panic codes:


CODE RED
Something needs to be solved on that day. Otherwise, some bad things may happen. Usually, everyone can be called via phone.


CODE YELLOW
Something needs to be solved, but it isn’t that urgent. It’s good to solve it in under 3 days.


CODE PURPLE
We have found a mistake/bug somewhere (graphic material, website…). It is good to solve it, but it isn’t that urgent. It is a problem that needs to be solved in 7 days or in a discussed time frame.


You will learn about things below later. Don’t worry if you don’t get it now.


ZenCycle
An active work phase for Zen Water lasting for 6 weeks in which we produce stuff after we have gone through our preparation plans for this ZenCycle, of course.


Chill Down
A passive phase for Zen Water in which we don’t think about Zen Water at all. This phase is 5 days long, and it comes after two successful ZenCycles.


Pitch
Do you have an idea? Make a pitch, and you will see if it gets approved by the Zen Water Team. If yes, we are going to work on it in next ZenCycle.


Kickoff
This is a summary of things that a team is going to work on on the next ZenCycle so everyone would know what is going to happen. Why do we do kickoffs? When it comes to project work, executing something the wrong way can destroy morale, grind the whole company down, erode trust, crunch gears, and wreck the machinery of long-term progress. So yeah, it’s “done,” but at what cost? That’s why we adjust our journey regularly.


Announcement
Would you like to tell everyone in the team without swinging between messages in the chat? Announcements in our system are meant to solve that problem. Let’s say you would close a deal with a company or a new employee is coming into the company. Make an announcement from it!


Heartbeat
An objective reflection on our previous ZenCycle. Did we succeed or not? We will see. Later on, you will get more details about those unknown words.
